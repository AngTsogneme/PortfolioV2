Tailored is a responsive platform where shoppers can customize various aspects of sweatshirts.

Why Tailored?

Due to the increased demand for online shopping during the pandemic, Tailored was designed to increase user satisfaction with online shopping.

Opportunities

After interviewing 5 participants and surveying 10, common problems were identified to be:

  • Dissatisfaction with product upon receiving.
  • Lack of information during shopping process.
  • Complicated return policies.

Users

"I want to buy clothes online and have it fit the way I expected."

Frustrations:
  • Annoyed when the product is not as expected.
  • Does not enjoy dealing with the return policies.
Goals:
  • Wants the clothes to look, feel and fit as expected so she does't have to deal with return policies.

How to make sure products meet user standards?

  • Allow users to define their own standards, alternatively allow users to customize their article of clothing.
  • Focused on one article of clothing (sweatshirts) to figure out what users should be able to customize in order to be satisfied.

Wireframes

After identifying user needs and narrowing down the goal of Tailored, wireframes, mockups and hi-fi prototypes were created to visualize and test the user journey.

Mockups

Test and Iterate

Key performance indicaters for the usability test were time on task, conversion rates, and user error rates.



Main changes after usability testing:

  • A less cluttered personalization process designed to decrease time on task and increase conversion rates.
  • Call to action buttons for providing instructions during the personalization process to decrease user error rates.

Next Steps

  • Expand into all articles of clothing. Research user expectations for other types of clothing.
  • Decrease the time it takes for the checkout process.
  • More usbaility testings.

Lessons Learned

  • It’s easy to get diverted from the initial problem so always keep the bigger picture in mind when making decisions.
  • Fitting too much content in one space can significantly reduce the appeal of your design, as well as the usability.

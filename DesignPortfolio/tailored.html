Tailored is a responsive platform where shoppers can customize various aspects of sweatshirts.

Why Tailored?

Due to the increase demand for online shopping, Tailored was designed to increase user satisfaction with online shopping.

Opportunities

After interviewing 5 participants and surveying 10 participants, common problems were identified to be:

  • Dissatisfaction with product upon receiving
  • Lack of information
  • Complicated return policies

Users

"I want to buy clothes online and have it fit the way I expected."

Frustrations:

  • Annoyed when the product is not as expected
  • Does not enjoy dealing with the return policies

How to make sure products meet user standards?

  • Allow users to define their own standards, alternatively allow users to customize their article of clothing.
  • Focus on one article of clothing and what aspects of that clothing piece should users be able to customize in order to be satisfied.

Mockups

Test and Iterate

Key performance indicaters for the usability test were time on task, conversion rates, and user error rates.



Main changes after usability testing:

  • A less cluttered personalization process designed to decrease time on task and increase conversion rates.
  • Call to action buttons for providing instructions during the personalization process to decrease user error rates.

Next Steps

  • Expand into all articles of clothing. Research what aspects to customize for other clothing pieces such that it meets user expectations.
  • Faster checkout process.
  • More usbaility testing.

Lessons Learned

  • It’s easy to get diverted from the initial problem so always keep the bigger picture in mind when making decisions.
  • Less is more in the context of fitting too much content in one space can significantly reduce the appeal of your design, as well as the usability.
